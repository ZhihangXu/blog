### 1. Split Gibbs Sampling, SGS
Goal: the posterior $p(x|y) \propto p(y|x)p(x) = \exp(-f(x;y) - g(x))$.

Instead of direct posterior sampling, SGS samples the **auxiliary distribution**:

<div>
$$\pi^{XZ}(x,z) \propto \exp\left(-f(z;y) - g(x) - \frac{1}{2\rho^2} \| x-z\|_2^2\right)$$
</div>

The SGS algorithm steps:
1) **Likelihood step**: 
<div>
$$z^{(k)} \sim \exp\left(-f(z;y) - \frac{1}{2\rho^2}\| x^{(k)}-z\|_2^2\right)$$
</div>
2) **Prior step**: 
<div>
$$x^{(k+1)} \sim \exp\left(-g(x) - \frac{1}{2\rho^2}\| x - z^{(k)}\|_2^2\right)$$
</div>
